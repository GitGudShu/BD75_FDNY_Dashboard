# Temporal Analysis: Volumetry & Trends

This report analyzes the temporal patterns of FDNY interventions (Fire and EMS). We examine yearly trends, seasonal variations, and daily/hourly cycles.

## 1. Yearly Evolution
The chart below shows the total number of interventions per year.

![Yearly Trend](/output/figures/temporal/yearly_trend.png)

*Key Observations:*
- **EMS Demand**: shows a steady trend.
- **Fire Incidents**: remain relatively stable or show slight variations.
- *Note*: Ensure 2025/2026 data is complete before interpreting drops in the final years.

## 2. Seasonality
We analyzed the average monthly volume to identify seasonal patterns.

![Monthly Seasonality](/output/figures/temporal/monthly_seasonality.png)

*Key Observations:*
- **Summer Peak**: There is often a noticeable increase during summer months.
- **Winter Dip**: Activity tends to be lower in February.

## 3. Weekly Patterns
The distribution of incidents across the week.

![Weekly Pattern](/output/figures/temporal/weekly_pattern.png)

*Key Observations:*
- **Weekdays vs Weekends**: Activity is generally higher during weekdays compared to weekends.

## 4. Hourly Cycle
The average daily cycle of operations.

![Hourly Pattern](/output/figures/temporal/hourly_pattern.png)

*Key Observations:*
- **Daytime Rise**: Usage climbs steadily from 6 AM.
- **Peak Hours**: Activity peaks in the late afternoon / early evening.
- **Night Lull**: The lowest volume is consistently found between 3 AM and 5 AM.
